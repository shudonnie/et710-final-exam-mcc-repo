🌱

Tax-free compounding

Unlike a taxable account, your Roth IRA grows without annual tax drag. Every dollar of growth stays invested and compounds — indefinitely.

🔄

Backdoor Roth

Earn too much to contribute directly? High earners can make a non-deductible traditional IRA contribution, then convert it to a Roth. Do this carefully with a tax advisor.

📋

Contribution flexibility

Roth IRA contributions (not earnings) can be withdrawn anytime, penalty-free. This makes it a better emergency backstop than most people realize.

🏢

Max your employer first

Always capture your full 401(k) match before contributing to an IRA. A 100% match is an instant 100% return — nothing beats it.

⚖️

Roth vs traditional

Roth wins if your future tax rate will be higher than today's. Traditional wins if you expect lower taxes in retirement. When unsure, Roth — tax-free growth is hard to beat.

⏱️

The five-year rule

Roth earnings can only be withdrawn tax-free after the account has been open 5 years AND you're 59½ or older. Contributions are always accessible.
